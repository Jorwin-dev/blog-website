The Blaugrana Run The Ballon d'Or!

Oct 31 2023 5 min read

What a night for Catalonia! The results of the 67th Ballon d'Or ceremony are in, and what a historic night it was! The 2023 Ballon d'Or Femenin winner is FC Barcelona & Spain's Aitana Bonmati, and the 2033 Mens Ballon d'Or winner is none other than the now 8-time winner, Inter Miami & Argentina's Lionel Messi.

Lionel Messi stunned us with yet another memorable club campaign, posting 20+ goals and assists in all competitions. But, his international campaign is what solidified his 2023 campaign as being worthy of a Ballon d'Or. He claimed the one trophy that alluded him, the one prize in the sport that every athlete dreams of winning, the biggest trophy in the world; The World Cup. After losing the first game of the tournament in a shocking result against Saudi Arabia, Argentina went on to put on masterful performances against top tier competition, spearheaded by the one and only Lionel Messi. Messi finished the competition with 7 goals, 3 assists, 21 key passes, 5 Player of the Match awards and the World Cup MVP award, with his best performance coming in the final against France where he scored twice and absolutely pulled the strings for 90+ minutes. Even when facing stiff competition from Man City & Norway's Erling Haaland who won the Treble and put up 70+ G/A; After finally capturing the award that alluded him since 2014 where he lost in the final against Germany, there was no doubt that the greatest of all time, made in La Masia, would win his 8th Ballon d'Or.

We then move to Aitana Bonmati, a player described by one of the greatest coaches of all time Pep Guardiola as "the female Andres Iniesta"; and man is he not kidding. Aitania is one of those players you have to watch to appreciate. She makes the game look so simple, almost as if in slow-motion; just like Iniesta. She accomplished something almost no other players have, she won the The Treble with FC Barcelona, winning UEFA Player of the Year for her stellar campaign with her club, and then she won the World Cup with Spain, winning the Women's World Cup MVP Awarad. She posted a career high 15 goals and 18 assists in her 2023 club campaign, and at just 25 years of age, we are in store to witness the prime years of one of the best female footballers the sport has ever seen.

Playing football is very simple, but playing simple football is the hardest thing there is. - Johan Cruyff

Cruyff, recognized as the most important figure in the history of Barca's philosophy said it best. The best players make it look easy; make it look simple. With two La Masia products, on opposite ends of the age spectrum, at different stages of their careers, both embodying the philosophies that define Barca as a club ensures the Culers that they are in good hands. Both Aitana & Messi put on masterful, otherworldly displays that embody what it means to be a Barca product, and what it means to make the game look so simple.
